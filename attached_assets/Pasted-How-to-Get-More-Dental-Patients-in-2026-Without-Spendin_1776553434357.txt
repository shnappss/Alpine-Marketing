How to Get More Dental Patients in 2026 (Without Spending More on Ads)

  Most dental practices in Switzerland don't have a marketing problem. They have a systems problem.

  They're spending money on ads. They have a Google Business Profile. Some even post on Instagram. But new
  patients aren't coming in at the rate the practice needs — and nobody can quite explain why.

  Here's why: getting more patients isn't about doing more marketing. It's about fixing the gaps between the
  marketing you're already doing and the moment someone books an appointment.

  This guide breaks down exactly what those gaps are, and how to close them — systematically.

  ---
  Why Word-of-Mouth Alone No Longer Works

  Ten years ago, a dental practice in Zurich or Basel could run almost entirely on referrals. A good
  reputation, a loyal patient base, and the occasional flyer in the letterbox.

  That model is broken.

  71% of consumers now search for a dentist online before booking — even when they've been personally referred.
   They're checking your Google reviews, looking at your website on their phone, and comparing you to three
  other practices before they make a decision.

  The practice that wins isn't always the best dentist. It's the one that looks most trustworthy online and
  responds fastest.

  The good news: this is entirely fixable with the right system.

  ---
  1. Know Exactly Who Your Ideal Patient Is

  Before you change anything about your marketing, get clear on who you're trying to attract.

  Not every patient is worth the same to your practice. A patient who comes in for a CHF 80 cleaning twice a
  year is very different from one who books implants, veneers, or Invisalign.

  Ask yourself:
  - What is the average treatment value of my ideal patient?
  - Where do they live? (Within 5km? 10km?)
  - What are they typically searching for when they find us?
  - What's the one thing we do better than any other practice nearby?

  Once you know the answers, every marketing decision becomes easier. You stop trying to attract everyone and
  start building a system that attracts the right people.

  Alpine note: We build what we call a Patient Profile before touching a single ad or piece of content for our
  dental clients. It saves thousands in wasted spend.

  ---
  2. Your Google Business Profile Is Your Most Important Marketing Asset

  If you do nothing else from this article, optimise your Google Business Profile.

  When someone searches "Zahnarzt Zürich" or "dentist near me," Google shows the local map pack before any
  website. Practices that appear in the top 3 of that map pack get the overwhelming majority of clicks.

  What the top-performing practices do differently:

  - Upload new photos every week — offices with 100+ photos get 520% more calls than those with fewer than 10
  - Respond to every review within 24 hours — both positive and negative
  - Post updates weekly — Google treats your Business Profile like a social media account
  - Use keywords in your business description — naturally include terms like "dental implants Zurich" or
  "Invisalign Basel"

  This costs nothing except 30 minutes per week. Most practices don't do it. That's your competitive advantage.

  ---
  3. Fix the Speed Problem (This Is Where Most Patients Are Lost)

  Here's what nobody tells dental practices about online leads:

  If you don't follow up within 5 minutes of an online enquiry, you lose 80% of those leads.

  Not 20%. Eighty.

  A potential patient fills out your contact form at 7pm on a Tuesday. Your receptionist sees it Wednesday
  morning and sends an email. By then, that person has already booked with someone else.

  This is the most common and most expensive problem we fix for dental practices. The solution is an automated
  follow-up system:

  1. Patient submits enquiry via website or ad
  2. Automated SMS goes out within 3 minutes: "Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out to [Practice Name]. We'll be
  in touch within the hour to confirm your appointment — is there a time that suits you best?"
  3. If no response in 24 hours, a second automated touchpoint goes out
  4. Your receptionist only handles leads that are already warm and engaged

  One of our clients — a general dental practice in canton Zurich — implemented this system and went from 12
  new patients per month to 31. Same ad spend. Same team. Just a faster, smarter follow-up system.

  This is what marketing automation actually means in practice. Not a complex software stack — a system that
  means no lead ever goes cold.

  ---
  4. Build a Reputation System (Not Just Ask for Reviews)

  Online reviews are not optional anymore. 92% of new patients read reviews before booking a dental
  appointment. A practice with 14 reviews and a 4.2-star rating loses to a practice with 200 reviews and a
  4.7-star rating — every time.

  But here's the mistake most practices make: they ask for reviews randomly, when they remember, and only from
  patients who seem happy.

  A reputation system does it automatically:

  - Patient leaves the practice after a positive appointment
  - 2 hours later, they receive an SMS: "We hope your visit was comfortable. If you have a moment, we'd really
  appreciate a Google review — it helps other patients find us: [link]"
  - Positive reviews go to Google. If a patient had a poor experience, they're directed to a private feedback
  form instead of a public review

  This isn't manipulation — it's good systems thinking. And it compounds over time. Six months of automated
  review collection transforms a 14-review profile into a 200-review profile.

  5. Social Media That Actually Brings in Patients

  Most dental practices either don't post on social media, or they post generic "Happy Monday!" content that
  generates zero patients.

  What actually works on Instagram and Facebook for dental practices in 2026:

  Before-and-after transformations — the single highest-performing content type for dental, consistently.
  Patients see a real result from a real person at your practice. Consent and anonymisation where needed — but
  these posts drive direct enquiries.

  "What to expect" process videos — short videos (60–90 seconds) of what happens during a cleaning, a whitening
   treatment, or an implant consultation. They remove fear. Fear is the #1 reason people avoid booking.

  Patient testimonials on video — filmed horizontally, natural lighting, no script. A patient talking directly
  to camera for 60 seconds about their experience at your practice is worth more than any ad you'll run.

  The dentist as a person — a 30-second video of you explaining something simple ("Why I always recommend X")
  builds trust faster than any promotional content. People buy from people.

  Post a minimum of 3 times per week. Engage with comments. Don't just schedule and disappear.

  ---
  6. Run Ads That Match Patient Intent

  If you're running Google Ads, the most important thing is matching your ads to what patients are actually
  searching.

  High-intent searches to target:
  - "dentist near me accepting new patients"
  - "dental implants [your city]"
  - "Invisalign consultation [your city]"
  - "emergency dentist [your city]"
  - "teeth whitening [your city]"

  Low-intent searches to avoid bidding on:
  - "how much do dental implants cost" (researching, not ready to book)
  - "dentist reviews" (comparison stage, not booking stage)

  A focused Google Ads campaign targeting high-intent searches in a 10km radius around your practice, with a
  landing page that matches the search term and an automated follow-up for every enquiry, is the single most
  predictable way to grow new patient numbers.

  Budget benchmark: Swiss dental practices should allocate CHF 800–2,500/month on Google Ads depending on their
   target treatment mix. High-ticket treatments (implants, Invisalign) justify higher spend because the patient
   lifetime value is significantly higher.

  ---
  7. Make Your Website Do the Work

  Your website has one job: convert a visitor into a booked appointment or an enquiry.

  Most dental websites fail at this. They look nice but they're not built to convert.

  The non-negotiables for 2026:
  - Mobile-first — 70%+ of dental searches happen on a phone
  - Click-to-call button visible immediately on mobile
  - Online booking or contact form above the fold — don't make patients scroll to find how to book
  - Social proof on the homepage — your Google rating and number of reviews, prominently displayed
  - Clear USP in the headline — not "Your friendly local dentist" but something specific: "Zurich's
  highest-rated practice for Invisalign and cosmetic dentistry"
  - Speed — if your website takes more than 3 seconds to load on mobile, you're losing patients before they
  even see it
